Colors

Color Components

Most DoodlePad Shape subclasses may be filled with one color and stroked (outlined) with a separate color. It is also possible to disable Shape fill or stroke, drawing shapes empty or without an outline. Colors in DoodlePad are constructed from color components: red, green, blue, and one transparency component: alpha. The level of each of the four components is specified with an integer in the range [0, 255].

Use the interactive widget on the right to explore how these four values are used to achieve a desired fill or stroke color. Drag the colored slider knobs up and down with the mouse to change a component value. The color that results from combining the four components is shown in the rectangular swatch on the right. Behind the swatch there are three colored lines. Transparency is increased by decreasing the alpha component value. Drag the alpha slider down to see the impact.

Pure red, green or blue is achieved by setting the corresponding color value to its maximum (255) and leaving the other two color values at 0. Other colors are constructed from different combinations.

  • Yellow:  {Red: 255, Green: 255, Blue:   0}
  • Orange:  {Red: 255, Green: 127, Blue:   0}
  • Cyan:    {Red:   0, Green: 255, Blue: 255}
  • Magenta: {Red: 255, Green:   0, Blue: 255}
  • White:   {Red: 255, Green: 255, Blue: 255}
  • Black:   {Red:   0, Green:   0, Blue:   0}
  • Gray:    {Red: 127, Green: 127, Blue: 127}
All shades of gray, from black to white, are formed by setting the values of red, green, and blue to equal values between 0 and 255.
